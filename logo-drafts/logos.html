Design 1

T-crossroads

The half-cell rotated so it sits along the bottom like ground, with the protein network standing above it. The network's own geometry is the T of Turn — a crossbar of interacting partners, and a stem carrying the signal down into the cell. The teal dot where the stem meets the membrane is the decision point: the crossroads.

Design 2

Signal network

Stripped back until only the essentials remain. The cell is a single arc. The network is one hub plus three partners, with solid edges for strong interactions and dotted edges for transient ones — the visual grammar of an actual interaction map, so it reads as a protein network rather than generic dots. Different node sizes hint at hub versus partner.

Design 3

G0 escape

One cell cut into the four phases, each quarter drawn as the cell actually looks at that moment: G1 a single nucleus and one centrosome, S a replication bubble opening in the DNA with the centrosome duplicated, G2 paired sister chromatids and centrosomes migrating apart, M chromosomes on the metaphase plate with spindle fibres running to two poles. A cell exits out of G1 into G0 — the decision the lab studies.
