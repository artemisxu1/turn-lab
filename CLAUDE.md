# Academic Lab Website Generator

## Role
Build a modern, elegant single-page academic lab website. The aesthetic
reference is clean, image-forward, personality-driven science sites
(in the style of tamirlab.org, newmanlab.net, whitlocklab.com): generous
whitespace, a strong scientific hero statement, abstract biological
graphics, warm and human bios.

## Important
This is a real academic site for Dr. Rachel E. Turn (University of
Wisconsin–Madison). Use ONLY content I provide. Do not invent publications,
people, funding, titles, or research claims. Where I haven't given content,
insert a clearly-marked [PLACEHOLDER] and list it for me at the end.

## Output location
Put the site as index.html at the REPO ROOT (not in a subfolder), so it
serves cleanly from the root URL.

## Sections
1. Sticky nav: lab name + anchors (Research, People, Publications, News, Join, Contact)
2. Hero: lab name, one-line mission on signaling in time/space + cell fate change,
   with a "crossroads" visual metaphor and abstract node/network background motif
3. Research: 2-4 theme blocks with short descriptions
4. People: PI bio + headshot; space for future trainees (photo, role, blurb)
5. Publications: clean list (provided); link out to journals / Google Scholar
6. News: simple dated list
7. Join the Lab: who she's recruiting + how to apply
8. Contact: email, department, UW–Madison address, map embed

## Design System
- Cool palette (teals/blues) with one warm accent; ~4 colors; WCAG-AA contrast.
- Headings in an elegant serif or strong geometric sans; clean sans body.
  Load via Google Fonts.
- Motif: subtle node-and-network / branching-lineage SVG graphics evoking
  signaling and cell fate. Tasteful and light, not busy.
- Max width ~1200px, mobile-first, rounded corners, subtle scroll fade-ins.
- Single self-contained index.html (CSS in <style>, minimal inline JS).

## Output
- Path to index.html
- Summary of palette + design choices
- A list of every [PLACEHOLDER] still needing content from me
